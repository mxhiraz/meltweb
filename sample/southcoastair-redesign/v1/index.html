FAQ

Answers to common questions

Everything you need to know about installation, servicing and getting the most from your system.

Still have a question?

Our team is happy to help — call us Monday to Friday, 8:30am–5pm.

Are you a licensed service company?
Yes. Southcoast Air is a licensed company and a member of the Australian Refrigeration Council (ARCtick). We hold licences PIC 43216, REC 26099, RTA AU35683 and RHL L109866.
What areas do you service, maintain, repair and install?
All Bass Coast areas — from Grantville, Bass, San Remo, Phillip Island, Wonthaggi, Leongatha, Cape Paterson, Inverloch and surrounds.
What type of equipment do you work on?
All types and brands of air conditioning, from a wall split system to ducted splits and package units — domestic and small commercial.
Who can service or repair my air conditioning unit?
Technicians must be ARC Tick approved to perform the work, ensuring they are qualified and hold the necessary licences and insurances.
How far from the wall should the outdoor unit be?
On the fan (front) side, allow a minimum of 1m for small splits and 2m for large package units. At the back, allow a minimum of 100mm for small splits and 200mm for large units.
Does maintenance & service reduce energy consumption?
Yes. Regular maintenance reduces breakdown costs, keeps the system running efficiently to lower energy consumption, and maximises equipment lifespan.
The outside unit drips water — is this normal?
It is completely normal for the outside unit to drip water, particularly when operating a window unit or running in heating mode.
How long does it take to install a new unit?
Split systems take 3+ hours, while ducted systems take 2–3 days, depending on any additional work required.
What temperature should I set the thermostat to?
Thermostats should be set between 18–20°C for heating and 24–27°C for cooling.
What is an inverter?
An inverter is a type of power-conversion circuit that provides optimum power control and extremely efficient operation by changing the frequency of the power supply.
