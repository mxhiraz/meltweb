FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Are you a licensed service company?
Yes. Southcoast Air is a licensed company and a member of the Australian Refrigeration Council (ARCtick) — licences PIC 43216, REC 26099, RTA AU35683 and RHL L109866.
What areas do you service?
All Bass Coast areas — from Grantville, Bass, San Remo, Phillip Island, Wonthaggi, Leongatha, Cape Paterson, Inverloch and surrounds.
What equipment do you work on?
All types and brands of air conditioning, from a wall split system to ducted splits and package units — domestic and small commercial.
Who can service or repair my unit?
Technicians must be ARC Tick approved to perform the work, ensuring they are qualified and hold the necessary licences and insurances.
How far from the wall should the outdoor unit be?
On the fan (front) side, allow a minimum of 1m for small splits and 2m for large package units. At the back, allow at least 100mm for small splits and 200mm for large units.
Does maintenance reduce energy consumption?
Yes — regular maintenance reduces breakdown costs, keeps the system efficient to lower energy consumption, and maximises equipment lifespan.
The outside unit drips water — is this normal?
It is completely normal for the outside unit to drip water, particularly when operating a window unit or running in heating mode.
How long does installation take?
Split systems take 3+ hours, while ducted systems take 2–3 days, depending on any additional work required.
What temperature should I set the thermostat?
Set thermostats between 18–20°C for heating and 24–27°C for cooling.
What is an inverter?
An inverter is a power-conversion circuit that provides optimum power control and extremely efficient operation by changing the frequency of the power supply.
